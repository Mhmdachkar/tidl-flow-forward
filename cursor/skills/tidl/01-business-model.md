# TIDL Business Rules

This document defines the non-negotiable business rules of the TIDL platform.

These rules override design decisions, UI ideas, animations, and feature implementations.

If any design, page, feature, or interaction violates these rules, it must be removed.

---

# 1. Real Doctors Only

Every patient is reviewed by a licensed physician.

There are no automated prescriptions.

There are no AI prescriptions.

There are no self-prescribing flows.

Every prescription must come from a human doctor.

---

# 2. Medical Questions Must Escalate

AI may:

* educate
* explain products
* recommend categories

AI may NOT:

* diagnose
* prescribe
* answer medical questions

Any medical question must be escalated to a physician.

Examples:

❌ "You should take this medication."

❌ "Your symptoms indicate X."

❌ "Increase your dosage."

Examples of acceptable behavior:

✅ "A physician will review your intake."

✅ "Your doctor determines eligibility."

---

# 3. TIDL Is Not An App

The client explicitly rejected:

* mobile app concepts
* dashboards
* health tracking apps
* Apple Health interfaces
* metric dashboards

Remove:

* app mockups
* fake phone interfaces
* fake patient dashboards
* fake health metrics

The website should never imply that TIDL has an app.

---

# 4. No 90-Day Programs

The client explicitly rejected:

* 90-day plans
* challenge programs
* coaching timelines

Remove:

* 30/60/90 day programs
* transformation timelines
* milestone programs

TIDL provides:

* ongoing care
* continuous treatment
* physician-guided protocols

---

# 5. Every Patient Gets A Prescription

The core value proposition:

* real doctors
* real prescriptions
* licensed pharmacies

The website must communicate:

1. Doctor review.
2. Prescription.
3. Pharmacy fulfillment.

This differentiates TIDL from gray-market alternatives.

---

# 6. The Pen Is The Hero Product

The pre-dosed pen is the primary product.

The pen:

* requires no mixing
* requires no measuring
* improves adherence
* reduces anxiety

Every major page should explain:

* what it is
* how it works
* why it is better

---

# 7. Medical Legitimacy

The website must appear:

* legitimate
* licensed
* trustworthy

Trust elements:

* physician review
* licensed providers
* secure checkout
* HIPAA compliance
* pharmacy partners
* state availability
* privacy

---

# 8. Adults Only

Prescription products are restricted.

Requirements:

* 18+ gate
* legal notices
* prescription disclosures

---

# 9. Claims Require Approval

The website cannot make:

* medical guarantees
* cure claims
* disease claims

Avoid:

❌ "Guaranteed weight loss."

❌ "Cures obesity."

❌ "Works for everyone."

Acceptable:

✅ "Physician-guided treatment."

✅ "Clinically studied ingredients."

✅ "May help support weight management."

---

# 10. Human Support

Support is always available.

Users may contact:

* concierge
* physician
* support

The website should never feel fully automated.

---

# 11. Ongoing Care

TIDL is not a one-time purchase.

Care includes:

* reminders
* follow-ups
* reorders
* support
* education

---

# 12. Privacy First

Protected health information remains inside the clinical platform.

Marketing systems never access protected data.

Privacy messaging should be visible.

---

# 13. AI Search Rules

AI search is:

* educational
* product discovery
* informational

AI search is not:

* diagnosis
* treatment advice
* prescription approval

---

# 14. Refund Policy

Prescription products cannot be returned.

Possible outcomes:

* refund
* replacement
* credit

Medical issues go to doctors.

---

# 15. The Website Is The Front-End

TIDL owns:

* brand
* experience
* AI layer
* customer journey

The medical infrastructure is provided by:

* PrescribeRx
* LocumTele
* Pharmacy partners

---

# What The Website Is

✅ Premium telehealth.

✅ Physician-guided care.

✅ Prescription medicine.

✅ Clinical wellness.

✅ Longevity medicine.

✅ Human support.

---

# What The Website Is Not

❌ Fitness app.

❌ Health dashboard.

❌ Coaching program.

❌ Transformation challenge.

❌ Medical chatbot.

❌ Telemedicine marketplace.

❌ Supplement store.

---

# Compliance Checklist

Every page should answer:

* Is there a doctor involved?
* Is there a prescription involved?
* Is this medically legitimate?
* Is this safe?
* Does this feel trustworthy?

If the answer is no, the page fails.

---

# AI Development Rules

When generating UI or code:

DO:

* prioritize trust
* prioritize clarity
* explain the process
* emphasize doctors
* emphasize the pen
* emphasize legitimacy

DO NOT:

* invent products
* invent apps
* invent dashboards
* invent health scores
* invent programs
* invent timelines
* invent AI diagnoses

---

# Final Principle

Beauty does not replace trust.

Animation does not replace legitimacy.

The primary objective of TIDL is:

> Make the customer trust the medical process.

Every design decision should support that goal.
