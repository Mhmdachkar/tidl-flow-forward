# TIDL Doctor & Pharmacy Experience

---

# Purpose

The physician and pharmacy workflow is the trust engine of TIDL.

The customer is not buying a supplement.

The customer is entering a legitimate medical process.

Every major page should reinforce:

- real physicians
- licensed providers
- prescription treatment
- regulated pharmacies
- ongoing care

The doctor and pharmacy experience must feel:

- transparent
- trustworthy
- professional
- reassuring

Not:

- anonymous
- hidden
- confusing

---

# Core Principle

TIDL does not sell medication directly.

The process is:

Assessment
→ Physician Review
→ Prescription
→ Pharmacy Fulfillment
→ Delivery
→ Ongoing Care

---

# User Understanding

The customer should understand:

1. A doctor reviews their case.
2. Treatment is not guaranteed.
3. Prescription eligibility is determined medically.
4. Medication is prepared by licensed pharmacies.
5. Ongoing care continues after delivery.

---

# Legal Requirements

Always disclose:

- physician review required
- prescription required

---

# Forbidden Patterns

Never imply:

- guaranteed approval
- instant prescriptions
- automatic medication

```text

```


# Success Metric

The patient should think:

> A real doctor is reviewing my information.

and

> This feels like legitimate medical care.

If the experience feels like buying a product online, this flow has failed.
