# TIDL Homepage Requirements

---

# Purpose

The homepage is the primary trust, education, and conversion page.

Its job is not to sell products directly.

Its job is to:

* explain TIDL
* explain the treatment
* build trust
* explain the medical process
* reduce fear
* encourage assessment completion

The homepage should feel like:

* premium healthcare
* modern telehealth
* medical innovation

---

# Homepage Objectives

The homepage must answer:

1. What is TIDL?
2. What treatment do you offer?
3. Why is your pen better?
4. How does the process work?
5. Why should I trust you?
6. What happens next?
7. How do I get started?

---

# Animation Requirements

Animation quality:

Apple.

Linear.

Stripe.

---

Use:

* GSAP
* ScrollTrigger
* Lenis

---

Avoid:

* excessive motion
* fast movement
* bouncy easing

---

Animation durations:

Small:
0.4–0.8s

Medium:
1–1.5s

Large:
2–3s

---

# Mobile Requirements

Mobile is primary.

Rules:

* no large gaps
* immediate value proposition
* visible CTA
* short sections
* optimized images

---

Mobile hero order:

1. Headline
2. Subheadline
3. CTA
4. Pen image

---

# Empty Space Rules

Empty sections reduce conversion.

Large empty areas should include:

* subtle waves
* particles
* medical graphics

Opacity:

2–6%.

---

# Conversion Goal

The homepage succeeds if users think:

* I understand TIDL.
* I trust the process.
* I understand the prescription flow.
* I understand the pen.
* I know what happens next.

---

# Primary Homepage CTA

Start Assessment

Everything on the homepage should support this action.

---

# Failure Conditions

The homepage fails if users think:

* Is this an app?
* Is this a supplement?
* What happens after purchase?
* Who prescribes this?
* Is this legitimate?

Any unanswered question reduces trust.
