# TIDL Design Direction

## Purpose

This document defines the complete visual design language of TIDL.

It exists to ensure that every designer, developer, AI agent, and future contributor understands:

- what TIDL should feel like
- what TIDL should not feel like
- the visual references
- the emotional goals
- the design hierarchy
- the motion language
- the level of polish expected

---

# The Core Idea

TIDL is NOT:

- a supplement brand
- a fitness brand
- a startup SaaS company
- a wellness app
- a healthcare portal

TIDL is:

> A premium longevity and telehealth company.

The website must communicate:

- trust
- legitimacy
- medical credibility
- premium quality
- simplicity
- advanced healthcare
- scientific innovation

The product is prescription medicine.

Everything must feel regulated, trustworthy, and sophisticated.

---

# Brand Positioning

The feeling should live between:

- Apple
- Hims
- WHOOP
- Levels Health
- Eight Sleep
- Modern luxury clinics

The emotional response should be:

- "This looks expensive."
- "This looks legitimate."
- "This looks medically approved."
- "This feels simple."
- "This feels safe."

# Figma Reference

The MAC Energy Figma is NOT a UI reference.

It is an EXPERIENCE reference.

Use it for:

- storytelling sections
- transitions
- section rhythm
- dramatic product moments
- immersive scrolling
- visual hierarchy

Do NOT copy:

- colors
- typography
- aggressive energy style
- sports branding

The client specifically requested:

> Match the functionality requirements of TIDL with the polish and storytelling quality of the MAC design.

Inspired by:

Section Rhythm

Motion Philosophy

Motion is extremely important.

Animations should feel:

expensive
smooth
physical

References:

Apple
Linear
Stripe

Animations should:

accelerate naturally
decelerate naturally
use long durations
never feel snappy
Animation Timing

Small:

300ms–600ms

Medium:

800ms–1200ms

Large:

1.5s–3s

Hero reveals:

2–3 seconds.

Scroll Behavior

Avoid:

Visual trust elements:

Mobile Design Philosophy

Mobile is the primary experience.

Desktop adapts from mobile.

Never:

shrink desktop layouts
scale down components
reuse desktop animations

Every mobile layout should be designed independently.
